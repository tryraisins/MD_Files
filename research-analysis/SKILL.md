# Research and Analysis

Turn an ambiguous research request into a bounded question, an explicit source contract, and an evidence-backed answer. Match the depth to the decision at hand.

## Choose the lens

- **Data research:** locate datasets, inspect provenance, define fields and quality limits, validate transformations, and distinguish observed values from inference.
- **Market research:** define audience, geography, segment, alternatives, pricing, adoption signals, and uncertainty; avoid treating marketing claims as market size.
- **Competitive intelligence:** compare named alternatives on the dimensions that matter to the user, cite primary evidence, and separate feature facts from interpretation.
- **Trend analysis:** time-bound the signal, compare multiple independent sources, identify drivers and counter-signals, and label forecasts as forecasts.
- **Search and discovery:** start with precise queries, refine with domain/date filters, prioritize primary sources, and record the exact page or dataset used.
- **Research analysis:** synthesize findings into themes, contradictions, confidence, implications, and next actions without hiding missing evidence.

## Workflow

1. Restate the question and define what would count as a useful answer.
2. Establish freshness, geography, audience, and source-quality requirements.
3. Gather primary sources first; use secondary sources for context and triangulation.
4. Extract only decision-relevant facts and preserve links, dates, and units.
5. Compare evidence, resolve contradictions, and label inference or uncertainty.
6. Deliver a concise synthesis with findings, caveats, and recommended next steps.

## Integrity rules

- Never invent sources, quotations, statistics, or current facts.
- Do not extrapolate a trend from one anecdote or one provider's marketing page.
- State when information is stale, unavailable, self-reported, or inferred.
- For high-stakes, current, or user-requested lookups, browse and cite the supporting sources.
